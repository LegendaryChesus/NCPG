Economics

In the thriving North Pole Community of Gamers, a unique economic system has emerged, fueled by the ingenuity and creativity of its residents. Here, the currency of choice is GGs, short for Gaming Goods, a versatile resource derived from the materials found in houses and computers. The residents of this extraordinary community earn their GGs through various means, including excelling at games, engaging in outdoor exercises, and contributing to the construction of houses. The heart of this bustling community lies in its commitment to individual empowerment and cooperation. Basic necessities such as food, exercise facilities, gaming amenities, and housing are provided for free, ensuring that every resident can lead a fulfilling life. To sustain this utopian environment, individuals contribute 30 GGs monthly, demonstrating their commitment to community values and harmony. One of the innovative enterprises driving the community's economy is the Goods Grower, a business that harvests snacks from the available resources and sells them to the community. The entrepreneurial spirit is not only encouraged but celebrated, as residents create unique products and services to exchange for GGs. The only constraints are adherence to community-approved rules, such as preventing monopolies, keeping prices under 300 GGs, and avoiding any harm to others. A standout success story is Heather Horton's company, Helmets for Hierarchy (HH). HH produces augmented reality helmets for an exhilarating real-life gun game, combined with outdoor exercise. The helmets offer various tiers of virtual weaponry based on the amount spent, encouraging healthy competition and physical activity. These innovative products are not just a source of entertainment but also contribute significantly to the community's economic vitality. The delivery of goods and services is facilitated by a third-party company, "zipline," which employs silent drones to transport products efficiently. Additionally, property ownership is made accessible to all, requiring a monthly fee of 100 GGs for a house. However, residents must establish a company generating at least 50 GGs in income to qualify for property ownership. While there is no fixed value assigned to possessions within houses, they can be resold, promoting a dynamic and ever-evolving marketplace. In this self-sustained and harmonious community, residents thrive by utilizing their skills, collaborating on innovative ventures, and embracing a shared commitment to a prosperous and equitable society. The North Pole Community of Gamers is a testament to the potential of a society driven by collective well-being and individual initiative.
